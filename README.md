# Excel-Project-Hospital-Administration-Patient-Outcome-Analysis-



## Project Description

This project presents an in-depth healthcare data analysis aimed at understanding patient readmission behavior and hospital stay patterns. Using a large dataset of more than **66,000 hospital patient encounters**, the study focuses on identifying key medical and demographic factors that influence readmission rates and recovery duration.

The analysis is designed to support **hospital administrators, healthcare managers, and policy makers** in improving patient outcomes, optimizing hospital resources, and reducing unnecessary operational costs.

---

## Business Problem Statement

Hospitals face continuous pressure to:

* Reduce avoidable patient readmissions
* Improve quality of patient care
* Manage hospital beds and staff efficiently
* Understand how medication changes affect recovery

High readmission rates often indicate gaps in treatment effectiveness, discharge planning, or post-hospital care. At the same time, longer hospital stays increase costs and reduce bed availability. This project addresses these challenges through structured data analysis.

---

## STAR Method Breakdown

### Situation

Healthcare institutions frequently deal with incomplete visibility into patient recovery patterns. Decision-makers often lack clear evidence on:

* Whether medication changes increase or reduce readmission risk
* How comorbid conditions affect length of stay
* Which patient groups require closer monitoring after discharge

Without data-backed insights, hospitals risk inefficient planning and compromised patient care quality.

---

### Task

The objectives of this project were to:

* Analyze hospital readmission trends using historical patient data
* Identify statistically significant factors linked to readmissions
* Evaluate the effect of diabetic medication changes on patient outcomes
* Measure how the number of medications influences total hospital stay
* Segment patients by demographic and risk groups to identify patterns

The goal was to convert raw healthcare data into meaningful, actionable insights.

---

### Action

A structured and methodical approach was followed throughout the analysis.

#### Data Cleaning and Preparation

* Addressed missing and inconsistent values in important columns such as **patient weight** and **medical specialty**
* Cleaned and standardized categorical variables for accurate grouping and comparison
* Ensured data reliability by removing invalid and incomplete records

#### Exploratory and Statistical Analysis

* Performed **Chi-Square tests** to examine the relationship between:

  * Medication changes
  * Hospital readmission status
* Applied **Linear Regression analysis** to study the correlation between:

  * Number of prescribed medications
  * Total time spent in the hospital

These techniques helped validate assumptions with statistical evidence rather than intuition.

#### Segmentation and Aggregation

* Used **Pivot Tables** to segment patients by:

  * Age group
  * Gender
  * Race
  * Comorbidity level (High-risk vs Low-risk)
* Compared average hospital stay durations across segments
* Identified patient groups with higher operational and medical risk

---

### Result

The analysis delivered strong and measurable outcomes:

* Patients who experienced **medication changes** showed a **statistically significant difference in readmission rates**, with a p-value less than 0.05

  * This finding supports the recommendation for **enhanced post-discharge monitoring** for these patients

* Patients in the **high comorbidity group** had a significantly longer average hospital stay:

  * High-risk group: **4.64 days**
  * Low-risk group: **2.60 days**

* These insights allow hospitals to:

  * Improve bed occupancy and staffing forecasts
  * Identify high-risk patients early
  * Design targeted care and follow-up strategies
